What Is a DD214 Form?

Your military discharge papers are officially known as DD Form 214, or just DD214. All veterans receive this Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty once they are out of the military.

In a nutshell: The DD214 is proof of your military service. All branches use this same form. There may be no more important document, as it serves as the key to unlocking benefits of all kinds.

What Is a Reserve DD214-1?

In recent years, the military's reserve components have also begun issuing departing reservists a new standard service record DD214-1, the Certificate of Uniformed Service, Reserve Component Addendum.

This form summarizes their periods of active and inactive service as well as the points they've earned toward retirement. Unlike active-duty members—who may receive only a single DD214 covering one continuous service commitment—reservists can accumulate many DD214s over a career, one for each activation and release.

Information the DD Form 214 May Include

Service Information

  • Active-duty entry date
  • Place of entry
  • Service length
  • Last duty assignment

Personal Details

  • Home address at entry
  • Mailing address after separation
  • Rank obtained
  • Military occupational specialty (MOS)

Honors & Education

  • Military education
  • Decorations, medals, badges
  • Citations and campaign awards
  • Foreign service

Separation Details

  • Separation type
  • Character of service
  • Authority/reason for separation
  • Eligibility codes

How to Get Your DD214

There are several ways to request a copy of your DD214:

🌐 Online Methods

Via https://vetrecs.archives.gov: The federal government's eVetRecs website is the fastest way to request your records online.

Via https://milconnect.dmdc.osd.mil: Visit the DMDC website for instructions on how to access your records via milConnect.

📧 Mail & Fax Methods

By Mail: Download the SF 180 (Request Pertaining to Military Records) and mail it to:

National Personnel Records Center (NPRC)

1 Archives Dr.
St. Louis, Missouri 63138

By Fax: Fax the completed SF 180 to the NPRC at 📞 314-801-9195

🏢 In-Person Options

  • Visit the National Personnel Records Center in person
  • Contact your state or county veterans agency
  • Hire an independent researcher
Processing Time: Your request may take 4-6 weeks or longer. Be patient and plan accordingly.

⚡ Fastest Way to Get a DD214

To receive a DD214 as quickly as possible:

  1. Visit the https://vetrecs.archives.gov website
  2. Go through the dropdown menus and select "Emergency Request" when you reach the Service and request details section
  3. Or fax SF 180 to 📞 314-801-9195 and specify the emergency in the Purpose section

Questions? Call 📞 314-801-0800, but note that hold times can be long.

Why Is a DD214 Important?

This separation document contains information normally needed to verify military service for benefits, retirement, employment and membership in veteran organizations. This form also gives civil and government agencies the information needed to apply any federal and state laws relevant to members who separate from the military.

Treat your DD214 like gold! This piece of paper is vital to many aspects of your life in the civilian world.

A DD214 Generally Is Needed For:

🏠 Housing

  • Home loans
  • VA housing benefits
  • Property tax exemptions

💼 Employment

  • Civilian employment
  • Federal job applications
  • Veteran hiring preferences

🎓 Education

  • GI Bill benefits
  • College admissions
  • Tuition assistance

🏥 Healthcare

  • VA medical services
  • Healthcare enrollment
  • Prescription benefits

💰 Financial

  • Social Security
  • Disability compensation
  • Unemployment benefits

🎖️ Other Services

  • Veteran organizations membership
  • Burial/flag services
  • Homeless veteran services
  • Military discounts

Government Agency Uses

Department of Veterans Affairs (VA): Uses the form to determine eligibility for specific benefits.

Department of Labor: Uses DD214 to determine eligibility for unemployment compensation and reemployment rights.

Important: Inaccurate information on the DD Form 214 could result in a delay or denial of benefits.

🎺 Funeral Honors

The surviving spouse of a military veteran also needs to know how to access the DD214 in order for the service member's funeral service to receive patriotic honors. These include the folding and presentation of the United States burial flag and the sounding of taps, at no cost to the family.

What If My DD214 Needs to Be Corrected?

Correcting a DD214 ensures that veterans' benefits, awards, and service history are accurately documented.

If veterans find an error on their DD214, they can request an official correction, which will be issued on a DD215 form.

The DD215 serves as an addendum to the DD214 and should be kept with it as part of the complete service record.

Why You Should Keep Your DD214 as Private as Possible

⚠️ Identity Theft Risk: The DD214 includes personally identifying information that could put you at risk for identity theft.

Someone with nefarious intent could apply for a credit card in your name or otherwise try to impersonate you. Identity thieves could use your personal information to obtain fraudulent credit, identification cards and other items, according to the U.S. Army's Human Resources Command.

🔒 Protection Guidelines

  • Treat as valuable property: Store your documents only in a safe location not accessible to strangers
  • Limit distribution: Only provide copies to authorized agencies and organizations
  • Secure storage: Use a fireproof safe or secure filing system
  • Digital copies: If scanning, encrypt files and use secure storage

Should I File a Copy at the Courthouse?

Over the years, experts have suggested that veterans file a copy of their DD214 at their local county courthouse, in order to make it easily accessible should the need arise.

Consider carefully: Before taking such a step, consider there may be a risk of identity theft if your DD214 would become publicly available, as many court records are.

Many municipalities have taken steps to ensure the privacy of veterans' records, either by preventing the general public from accessing discharge papers, or by blacking out some identifying information. Do your research before filing a copy of your paperwork at the courthouse.

✅ Best Practices

DO

  • Keep in secure, fireproof location
  • Make certified copies when needed
  • Encrypt digital versions
  • Research courthouse privacy policies

DON'T

  • Leave in unsecured locations
  • Share on social media
  • Email unencrypted copies
  • Give to unverified organizations

Replacing Lost Military Records

Have you lost important military records and don't know how to replace them? Learn what to do if you lose your discharge, separation, or other papers.

You can also request records online via https://vetrecs.archives.gov or the National Archives.

📝 Using Standard Form 180

The paperwork involved in requesting records replacement is relatively simple using Standard Form 180:

  1. Download: Get a copy of Standard Form 180, Request For Military Records
  2. Type or Print: Remember to either type or print on the form
  3. Section 1 (Blocks 1-4): Fill in the individual's name, social security number, date and place of birth
  4. Section 1 (Block 5): Provide the dates of service
  5. Section 1 (Block 6): Indicate whether or not the individual is deceased
  6. Section 1 (Block 7): Indicate whether or not the person retired from the military
  7. Section 2: Indicate which medals need to be replaced
  8. Section 3: Record a return address and sign (only the service member, guardian, or next of kin may sign)
Include if possible: A copy of the discharge or separation document, WDAGO Form 53-55 or DD Form 214.

📮 Mailing Instructions

Mail the completed SF 180 and supplementary documents to the appropriate address, as detailed on the back of the form.

Processing Time: The National Archives and Records Administration receives many requests, and a response may take six months or more.

💡 Important Notes

  • It is not necessary to request a duplicate copy of a veteran's discharge or separation papers solely for the purpose of filing a claim for VA benefits
  • If complete information about the veteran's service is furnished on the application, VA will obtain verification of service from the National Personnel Records Center or the service department concerned
  • You are entitled to your military records free of charge
  • Be aware of advertisements that claim you have to pay to get your DD214, or that they can get it more quickly—they can't
